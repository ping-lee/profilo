But even with a conservative Supreme Court, a Republican-led Senate and Republican-led congressional delegations in most states, election law experts are confident this won’t happen.

## 中国主导签署贸易协定，美国国际地位受挑战

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        After eight years of talks, China and 14 other nations from Japan to New Zealand to Myanmar on Sunday formally signed one of the world’s largest regional free trade agreements, a pact shaped by Beijing partly as a counterweight to American influence in the region. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      经过八年的谈判，中国与日本、新西兰、缅甸等14个国家于周日正式签署了世界上规模最大的区域自由贸易协议，该协议由中国主导，在一定程度上是为了平衡美国在亚太地区的影响力。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        The agreement, the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership, or <Text as="mark">R.C.E.P.</Text>, is limited in scope. Still, it carries <Text as="ins">considerable symbolic heft</Text>. The pact covers more of humanity — 2.2 billion people — than any previous regional free trade agreement and could help further cement China’s image as the dominant economic power in its neighborhood.
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      这个名为《区域全面经济伙伴关系协定》（Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership，简称RCEP）的协议涉及的范围有限。尽管如此，它仍具有相当大的象征意义。该协定覆盖了22亿人口，比以往任何一个区域自由贸易协定覆盖的都更多，有助于中国进一步巩固其作为周边占主导地位的经济强国的形象。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        It also comes after a <Text as="ins">retreat</Text> by the United States <Text as="ins">from</Text> sweeping trade deals that reshape global relationships. To some trade experts, this new agreement shows that the rest of the world will not wait around for the United States. As other countries sign new deals, American exporters may gradually lose ground. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      在美国退出重塑全球关系的全面贸易协定之后，各国才签署这一协议。在一些贸易专家看来，RCEP的签署表明，世界上的其他国家不会空等着美国回归。随着其他国家签署新的协议，美国出口商可能会逐渐处于不利地位。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>


<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        Mr. Biden has said he would <Text as="ins">wait to</Text> negotiate any new trade deals. He wants to focus his energy on the pandemic, the economic recovery and investing in American manufacturing and technology. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      拜登已表示，他目前不会就任何贸易协定进行谈判。他希望把精力集中在控制疫情、复苏经济，以及投资美国制造业和技术上。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

## 领跑世界的新冠疫苗背后的科研伉俪

## 用黑胶唱片重现被遗忘的精品

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        Around the world, artists are enjoying a resurgence of popularity thanks to the boom in reissues. Record labels and curators are increasingly devoting significant resources to re-releasing tunes from earlier decades on vinyl, CD, cassette and on digital formats, in the hope of turning them into collectors' items. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      全球范围内，一些艺术从业者因作品再版的风靡而再度走红。唱片公司和策展人们越来越多地倾注重要资源，将几十年前的音乐以黑胶、光盘、卡带或数字格式重新发行，希望把它们变成收藏者的藏品。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        <Text as="ins">Whilst</Text> the bulk of reissues <Text as="ins">tend to be</Text> classic albums, such as the Beatles' Abbey Road, a small but growing selection is devoted to <Text as="ins">obscure cuts</Text> of funk, psychedelia and traditional music from the developing world. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      尽管再版的唱片大部分是经典作品，比如披头士乐队的《艾比路》，但也有一些是小众的发展中国家的放克、迷幻、传统音乐集锦，这些唱片的数量正在增长。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>


<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        Reissues aren't a new phenomenon, but there's been an <Text as="ins">undeniable increase</Text> in recent years, amid the robust demand for vinyl. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      再版并不是一个新现象，但近年来，伴随市场对黑胶唱片的强劲需求，再版的趋势肉眼可见地在增长。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        Last year, for the 14th consecutive year, album sales on wax hit new highs in the US. It's a similar story in the UK, where vinyl LP sales have increased for 12 consecutive years, with 2019 marking the highest level of demand since the early 1990s. As a result, prices of limited-edition reissues have <Text as="ins">soared</Text>. 
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      去年，黑胶唱片在美国的销量连续第14年创下新高。英国的情况也类似，黑胶唱片销量连续12年增长，2019年达到了自20世纪90年代初期以来黑胶唱片需求的最高水平。因此，限量再版的价格飙升。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

<Accordion allowToggle fontSize="19px">
  <AccordionItem borderStyle="none!important" mb="0.5rem">
    <Flex>
      <Box flex="1" textAlign="left">
        Some experts describe this as a chicken-and-egg situation: On one hand, you have dedicated labels that invest a great deal of blood, sweat and tears into reissues. <Text as="ins">But on the other hand, reissues have gotten so trendy that they inevitably pave the way for countless regurgitations that eventually saturate the marketplace.</Text>
      </Box>
      <AccordionButton w="none" p={0} bg="none!important" boxShadow="none!important">
        <AccordionIcon />
      </AccordionButton>
    </Flex>
    <AccordionPanel px={0}>
      一些专家把这个情况形容为“鸡生蛋还是蛋生鸡的难题”：一方面，市场创造出了倾注大量汗泪心血制作而成的专属再版唱片；另一方面，这样的重新发行又变成一种流行趋势，从而促使市场创造更多的再版唱片。这样的反发行趋势不可避免，最终会使市场饱和。
    </AccordionPanel>
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
